Minerals

There major minerals, which are required in amount of at least 100mg/day. They are sodium, potassium, chloride, phosphorus, calcium, magnesium, and sulfur

Calcium
Functions: Major component of bones and teeth, Required for muscle contractions, Required for nerve transmission, Plays a role in cellular metabolism, Aids blood clotting

Magnesium
Function: Assists enzymes in more than 300 chemical reactions in body, Supports cellular activity, Participates in muscle contraction, Aids blood clotting, A component of bone

Sulfur
Functions: Helps maintain acid-base balance Assists in some of liver’s drug detoxifying pathways Component of some vitamins and AAs

There are also trace minerals, these are required in amounts less than 100mg/day. They are chromium, copper, fluoride, iodine, iron, manganese, molybdenum, selenium, zinc. Iron
Functions:Carries O2 throughout body Assists in energy metabolism and other enzyme-mediated chemical reactions Supports immune function Involved in production of neurotransmitters (chemicals that carry messages between nerve cells) Participates in development of brain and nervous system

Chromium
Functions: Enhances effects of insulin – plays role in development of glucose intolerance and type II diabetes

Copper:
Functions: Assists with transport of iron

Fluoride:
Function: Helps prevent dental caries (decay or cavities) – nearly 99% of body’s fluoride resides in bones and teeth

Iodine:
Function: Component of thyroid hormones – thyroid regulates metabolic rate and body temperature

Manganese:
Function: Important in many enzyme-mediated chemical reactions – enzymes involved in synthesis of cartilage in skin and bone

Molybdenum:
Function: Assists several enzymes – including one required for metabolism of sulfur- containing amino acids

Selenium:
Function: Needed for immune function & for synthesis of thyroid hormones Assists enzymes in protecting cell membranes from damage

Zinc
Function:Critical for normal growth and sexual maturation Places role in immune system Important to proper function of at least 70 enzymes including one that helps protect cells from damage
